G20 Summit

Overview

The G20 (Group of Twenty) is an international forum for the governments and central bank governors from 19 countries and the European Union. It was established in 1999 with the aim of discussing and coordinating global economic policies. The G20 represents around 85% of the world's GDP and two-thirds of its population.

Functions

  • Promoting international financial stability and sustainable economic growth.
  • Facilitating policy coordination among member countries.
  • Addressing global economic challenges and promoting reforms.
  • Engaging in dialogue with international organizations and stakeholders.

Members of G20

  • Argentina
  • Australia
  • Brazil
  • Canada
  • China
  • France
  • Germany
  • India
  • Indonesia
  • Italy
  • Japan
  • Mexico
